The Challenge

Unified Platform Launch

Numerous client OEM portals had been built on different technology over the years. Development teams were siloed and distant based on technology stacks. Duplication of talent and effort resulted in avoidable development costs and delay all the while missing the benefit of synergistic collaboration.

To streamline operations and improve time to market for clients, the Project Management Office developed an 18-month plan for migration of all clients to a common core multi-tenant platform.

Additional Challenges

Adobe Flash EOL &
New Client Win

One key feature, Epsilon’s Campaign Builder for Direct Mail and Email was an existing feature for some OEM portals and a desired roadmap feature for others. Flash was a key technology used in the Editor feature for the Campaign Builder. In July 2017, Adobe announced the end-of-life (EOL) for Flash Player by December 31, 2020. Replacing Flash would cause a complete rearchitecture for the Campaign Builder lenghting the timetable for the feature’s migration to the new platform.

Epsilon also was successful in winning a new OEM client. To allow the new client to launch on the new platform, the project timeline was pulled in by 6 months. To meet this new timeline, the Editor portion of the Campaign Builder was descoped for the initial platform launch.

Discovery

Blueprints to Interviews

At the onset of the project, the budget was not available for client field visits. Acknowledging this constraint, I performed a series of mini-discovery sessions with key stakeholders and the Senior Leadership Team to identify upstream and downstream processes, user roles, groups and resources.

Using a Service Blueprint to communicate and gain clarity on the initial findings, I drafted a User Research plan and developed Interview Protocols for each user persona.

Design Methods

User Studies

I performed user studies with internal users (Account, Sales / Field Reps, Production Support, Program Headquarters and Key stakeholders) synthesizing findings into Insights and Design Principles.

Competitive Analysis

The Strategy, Product, Business, Engineering and the UX/UI Teams first performed competitive analysis of various competitive offerings. We also analyzed the requirements of the new OEM Client for the portal.

Exploring Concepts

I started with a sitemap and user flows for the new portal. After exploring several wireframe concepts, I built an initial Axure prototype. I then held participatory design sessions reviewing the prototype with key stakeholders from Sales and Program Support teams. Using real-time feedback, I refined both the design and prototype as well as gaining internal alignment on the UX and UI.

Prototyping & Final Approvals

Client Workshops

After gaining internal alignment, I traveled to the client’s North America Headquarters with representatives from Account, Product Management and our Senior Leadership Team for 3 days of onsite collaborative design sessions. During this time, I used prototyping to validate revisions, received final approvals on the design while building a rapport with our clients.

Happy Client,
Successful Launch

During the project, a strong foundation was laid with our new client while building internal bridges between groups (Engineering, Account, Product Management, Support, Sales, and Program Support).

Future Enhancements

An Exciting Roadmap!

After the platform launch, the team started work on the minimum viable product solution for the Editor portion of the Campaign Builder. Feedback received from dealers and clients underscored a desire for an omni-channel Campaign Builder. A new omni-channel solution that supports traditional Direct Mail and Email channels as well as Digital Channels (Search, Social and Online Advertising) is being researched, designed and tested.
