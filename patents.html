Social Networking

1

US 8620996
 Granted 12-31-2013

Determining group preference in a Social network

Determine a group preference in a social network from multiple individual preferences of members of the social network is provided. A combination of an individual's importance to a social network and a social network's importance to the individual as weighting factors when combining the individual preferences to generate a shared set of preferences used to select content for broadcast to the network, including audio content and video content. User Interface & Control

2 Patents

1

US 9436301
 Granted 09-06-2016

Portable electronic device having interchangeable user interfaces

A portable device comprising a housing, a first User Interface (UI), a second UI and one or more sensors, and a method thereof. The first UI has an active state, and the second UI has an inactive state while the first UI is in the active state. The sensor or sensors detect an environmental condition or energy level. The second UI changes from the inactive state to the active state and the UI changes from the active state to the inactive state in response. View Patent

2

US 9354714
 Granted 05-31-2016

Keypad with integrated touch sensitive apparatus

A touch sensitive keypad device for a device includes one or more keys situated in a keypad carrier. One or more conductive layers span the keys. One or more transverse conductive layers are integrated with a non-key component, such as the keypad carrier. A capacitance sensing circuit detects changes between the conductive layers and the transverse conductive layers to detect user input, thereby allowing a user to use a matrix of keys as a touch pad. View Patent

User computer device with temperature sensing capabilities and method of operating same

Device with a temperature sensitive user interface that generates thermal patterns that may be transferred to other thermally sensitive electronic devices or for authentication. The device can auto-bias a temperature for optimization and can detect and thermally communicate with a thermal energy docking station and, based on thermal recognition, activate applications displayed on the temperature sensitive touchscreen. Capacitive & Thermal Stylus Devices

3 Patents

1

US 9110534
 Granted 08-18-2015

Stylus devices having variable electrical characteristics for capacitive touchscreens

Devices and methods of stylus devices that when in contact with a touchscreen and a grounding element, can vary the signal level detected on a touchscreen to create a more realistic writing experience. The resistive link between the touchscreen and a grounding element can be varied to enable features such as varying the width of line being drawn, to vary a region of influence on the touchscreen and/or to vary a temperament of action. View Patent

Active styluses for interacting with a mobile device

An active stylus or a method performed for interacting with a device wherein the device has at least one sensor (e.g. a thermocouple junction) and wherein the stylus has at least one signal source (e.g. an analog heat source) that produces at least one signal (e.g. at least one analog temperature signal) wherein the at least one signal is configured to be detectable/variable by the at least one sensor of the device. View Patent
