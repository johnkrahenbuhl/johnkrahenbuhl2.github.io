Pioneering Design School

Professor Emeritus & IIT

On a crisp fall Chicago afternoon, I first met Professor Vijay Kumar who leads the Strategic Design Thinking and Design Methods Programs at Illinois Institute of Technology's Institute of Design (ID). I was looking into Master’s Programs and was attending one of Professor Kumar’s Design Workshops for prospective students of ID.

The energy in the classroom was exhilarating and reminded me of the positive energy I felt as a member of a successful patent generation team at Motorola. I knew I had found a collaborative environment to refine my skills in user centered design and innovation methods and was excited to see what the future held in store.

The Challenge

The Farmer & The Venture Capitalist

One of my initial courses was a Design Planning Workshop ran by Prof. Kumar based on the application of methods and a structured innovation process described in his book “101 Design Methods”.

For our workshop, our client and sponsor was a Venture Capital Research firm located in the Mid-West. Over 16 weeks, a team of 7 Graduate students would fulfill dual roles as researchers and workshop facilitators while focusing on the innovation planning process for user-driven and business-driven innovation the agricultural space.

Design Planning

Innovation Process & Approach

The Innovation Process model is a 2 x 2 where quadrants map to modal categories of Research, Analysis, Synthesis and Realization. We focused on 5 modes of the Design Innovation Model and their associated goals and activities as outlined below:


Adapted from "101 Design Methods", John Wiley & Sons Publisher

  1. Sense INTENT

    • Map Trends & State Initial Intent

  2. Know CONTEXT

    • Plan for Research & Map Evolution

  3. Know PEOPLE

    • Surveys, Interviews & Workshops

  4. Frame INSIGHTS

    • Find Insights, Patterns & Flows

  5. Explore CONCEPTS

    • Convert Insights into Design Principles
    • Define, Organize & Communicate Concepts

Design Methods

Online Survey

After finalizing our Statement of Intent, we developed an Online Survey to gather primary data from farmers on technological trends, what farmer's perceive to be their biggest challenges and to identify candidates for field visits.

We had 10 Mid‑Western farmers respond to our survey. 62% responded that Farm Management Software would have the greatest impact on farming, 55% saw Financial Risk as the biggest challenge and 5 out of 10 agreed to field visits.

User Interviews

We developed two sets of interview protocols. The first protocol was narrow in focus developed for Subject Matter Experts (SMEs). A second core protocol had a broader focus targeting individual farmers and focused on themes such as technology, purchasing behaviors, goals/aspirations, challenges, and marketing/distribution.

Prior to performing User Interviews, all team members completed the National Institutes of Health "Protecting Human Research Participants" certification. After receiving our certifications, the team of Graduate Students divided into two smaller teams and performed 8 interviews total (3 SME and 5 farmer interviews).

Field Visits

The teams were able to take advantage of the 5 field visits offered. During our observational research, we were able to collect audio, video, and photographic evidence as well as perform additional in person interviews.

Design Principle Generation & Concept Exploration

Co‑Design Workshop & Capstone

Leveraging the data from the primary and secondary research, we were able to use various additional tools to develop 11 key insights and design principles. We used these insights and design principles to prepare an innovation brief and guide our planning activities for a one‑day Co‑Design Workshop with our client.

More than a dozen SMEs from various business units, students, faculty and farmers were in attendance. Individual teams were facilitated by Graduate students, cross‑functional client representatives as well as farmers. The teams came up with 6 concepts highlighting areas for further exploration.
